Ink is for sites where the post is the product. The masthead stays quiet,
the column is narrow, and the typeface is a book serif — not because
sans-serif is wrong for blogs, but because long-form reading rewards a
page that doesn't compete with the prose.

The author box at the end of a post is intentional too: after a few
thousand words, a face and a short bio are a better next step than another
row of share buttons.

None of that requires a faithful Ghost Attila port. Ink borrows the mood —
centered title, serif body, restrained chrome — and implements it as a
Dune-native theme with the usual templates, search, and config panel.
